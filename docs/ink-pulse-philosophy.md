# Ink Pulse — 墨脉

## Design Philosophy

Form speaks through the weight of ink. Every line is a declaration—thick where emotion surges, thin where thought trails off. The 4px border is not decoration; it is the skeleton of the page, the bone structure beneath the skin of information. Shadows do not soften—they are hard-edged companions, offset by exactly 5 pixels, as if each element has been stamped onto the page with the force of a woodblock press. Double shadows create depth not through blur but through accumulation: two distinct offsets, two declarations of presence. This is the product of painstaking attention to the physics of printed matter, where shadow is not gradient but solid.

Halftone is the breath of the page. Dots at 4px intervals create a texture that vibrates between presence and absence—close enough to suggest tone, far enough to reveal the grid beneath. Three densities operate as a tonal system: whisper (0.03 opacity) for atmosphere, speech (0.08) for structure, shout (0.15) for emphasis. The dots invert with the theme: black on cream in daylight, white on charcoal at night. This chromatic inversion is not an afterthought but a meticulously crafted dual-reality, as if the same page exists simultaneously in two printing processes. Every density calibrated through countless proofs by a master of print reproduction.

Speech takes shape. The voice bubble speaks with a triangular tail pointing to its source. The thought bubble thinks in cloud-form—overlapping circles softening certainty into contemplation. The shout bubble explodes with jagged edges, each tooth of the polygon a small rupture in composure. The whisper bubble dissolves its border into dashes, its text shrinking to match its reticence. These four forms are not decorative choices but a meticulously crafted typographic system as rigorous as any classical grammar—each shape carrying semantic weight, each silhouette a word before the word appears. Onomatopoeia erupts at 3 to 5 rem, outlined in ink-stroke, rotating into existence from nothing.

The page tilts. Cards rotate between negative five and positive five degrees—not randomly, but as a compositional rhythm that directs the eye across the surface like panels in a manga spread. Overlap is permitted, even encouraged: z-index becomes narrative depth, foreground and background telling different moments of the same story. Torn edges at the top of each card reference the physical act of tearing—a rejection of digital smoothness in favor of material truth. Concentration lines radiate from moments of impact. Motion lines trail behind movement. Impact frames flash and expand. The paper itself yellows slightly (#FDF8E8), carries the faint tremor of ink on fiber, folds at the midpoint of long passages as if someone once carried this knowledge in their pocket. Every texture, every angle, every shadow offset is the result of deep expertise in translating the tactile language of comics into the precision of code.
